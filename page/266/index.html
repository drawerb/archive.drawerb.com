UPDATE: Led Zeppelin reunion rumors bollocks

Robert Plant has shot down the news story that’s been circulating all week that claimed the remaining members of Led Zeppelin were reforming for a memorial concert honoring Atlantic Records’ founder Ahmet Ertegun. Plant said he has heard nothing about such a show, adding, “If there was one, then there wouldn’t be enough doctors to support it!” True enough. And I imagine that perishes the thought of there ever being one. It’s way too late, anyway.

Boredoms staging one-of-a-kind show on 07/07/07

Japanese noise experimentalists Boredoms have always taken the road dared to be traveled, and the band’s plans for July 7, 2007 are no exception. With 77 drummers in tow, the band will perform 77BOADRUM as a one-time-only, free show in the Empire-Fulton Ferry State Park section of Brooklyn Bridge Park. 74 players from New York’s avant-garde scene, which includes members of Gang Gang Dance, No Neck Blues Band, Sunburned Hand Of The Man, Excepter, Sightings,

and Oneida, were chosen by the event’s music director, Hisham Bharoocha. It will be a live sculpture built out of musicians, as Boredoms will serve as the centerpiece surrounded by coiling layers of drumkits. The impetus for the show stems from the significance of the number 7 in Japanese culture.

For more information: http://www.viceland.com/77BOADRUM

Lily Allen already at work on second record

Having just finished all of her obligatory appearances in the US, Lily Allen has holed up in a New York City apartment to write the follow-up to her breakthrough, Alright, Still. She’s again working with production duo Futurecut, but she’s in no hurry to get any product out, saying: “The songs I’ve written so far are good, they just don’t shout anything different to me than what the last album was like and I’d like to do something different.”

Allen is scheduled to appear at the Concert for Diana on Sunday July 1 at the new Wembley Stadium in London.

Depeche Mode’s Dave Gahan to release new solo album

Depeche Mode frontman, Dave Gahan, is set to release his second solo album, entitled Hourglass this October. It’s the follow-up to 2003’s respectable offering, Paper Monsters, which proved that Gahan could actually write a tune without Martin Gore’s ever-presence.

Gahan on the new record:

“We were very aware of the importance of keeping urgency in the sound and a feeling of spontaneity. We didn’t want to get bogged down in trying to make everything sound perfect. You want to keep the rough edges.”
