Weezer camp denies curious url redirection hinting at a possible new record

Rolling Stone is reporting some weirdness with the url albumsix.com, which apparently redirects over to weezer.com. Predictably, Weezer’s publicist denies any involvement in the curious interweb shenanigans.

Rivers Cuomo left the future of the band deliberately vague after the tour supporting its fifth album, Make Believe, so that he could finish his degree at Harvard and get married.

Interscope Records registered the domain in question, which is under the same Universal Media Group umbrella as Geffen, Weezer’s label. If the band had any sense it would cut its losses and pack it in for good, since its last decent record was Pinkerton, released way back in 1996.

REVIEW: Battles, Mirrored, Warp

After seeing Ian Williams play guitar with Don Caballero on the What Burns Never Returns tour, I told anyone within earshot that I wanted to have his babies. Now, I realized this did not follow the natural order of things, as we were both men. I said it in jest openly and loudly, and I probably meant it at that moment on some level or another. It was more of a hyperbolic reaction to his guitar playing, which was so completely bad ass it made my brain hurt. And the fact that he just smacked on gum, effortlessly tapping his way through various time signatures was too much to process.

Reznor rips label for over-charging

Trend Reznor is pissed off at Universal Music Group for pricing Nine Inch Nails’ current Year Zero well above standard retail:

“As the climate grows more and more desperate for record labels, their answer to their mostly self-inflicted wounds seems to be to screw the consumer over even more.”

Then, in the ultimate kiss-off, he slaps them with a “shame on you.” Did he suddenly turn into my great aunt? Shame on you? What the ass is that about? How’s that going to teach them? I expect this guy to piss burning fire from the depths of his black soul all over their pagan faces, not slap them with a limp-wristed ought-niner-ism.

Poor Lily Allen feels ‘fat, ugly and shittier than Winehouse’

Mouthy pop-singer Lily Allen has been pushing the limits of her charm lately. First canceling her tour out of sheer laziness, and now caving in to pressure to be extremely thin. Allen posted an embarrassing blog (amusingly titled ‘fat, ugly and shittier than Winehouse’) on her Myspace page about how she had “fallen victim to the evil machine.” Researching gastric bypass surgery in a “sea of tears” from her hotel room in Seattle, Allen admitted she isn’t quite as strong and independent minded as she’d always thought. One day’s worth of ingratiatingly supportive comments from fans on her Myspace page has cheered her up enough to apologize with another blog: “I’ve been reading all your comments and they have made me realize there is more to life than being thin! So thank you.” It took comments from a bunch of sycophants to realize this? Allen has quickly morphed from a tough-as-nails shit-talker to an insecure, spoiled little girl in a matter of weeks. She’s still pretty fucking cute, ‘fat’ and all.

Bowie and Sting- not above strippers

David Bowie and Sting have teamed up with club owner Ivan Kane to open a glitzy Burlesque club in New York City. Sting, long-rumored to be a frequent visitor of VIP rooms, might as well own a strip club so he can control the flow of gossip surrounding his new age-y marital rules. With the trio having plonked down 4 million to renovate an old restaurant space, the high-end gentelman’s club will have “100 percent of the focus to be on the girls and the dancing.” I’m glad their standards are high. It’s a delicate art, operating a strip club.
