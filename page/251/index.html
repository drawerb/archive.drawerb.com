More Cat Power covers

I’m not a huge fan of cover records. A cover every now and again is ok, but entire albums tend to be lame and indulgent, not to mention a transparent stop-gap at the cash register between real albums. Saddle Creek posts two new Mp3’s by The Good Life

Tim Kasher’s less caustic outfit, The Good Life, has a new record entitled Help Wanted Nights out September 11 on Saddle Creek, which has posted just two new songs for download. If these songs are indicative of the rest of the record, then it’s destined to be a bit of a downer. It’s by no means as emotionally wrenching as his darkest Cursive material- just more resigned and listless with a classic-rock-ish vibe.

M.I.A. shows her ass in interview

M.I.A. kind of loses it in an interview with Pitchfork. Immediately after exchanging pleasantries and ordering pancakes she gets pretty confrontational with the interviewer, who is obviously caught off guard. She’s extremely defensive about credit for her records going to her producers, particularly Diplo, which she blames Pitchfork for insinuating. The interviewer holds his own, though, even pressing her to defend herself. It’s a thoroughly entertaining read.

Thrill Jockey celebrates 15 years with London shows, box set

Staunchly independent Chicago record label Thrill Jockey will celebrate its 15th birthday with a pair of shows at London’s Kiko on November 11-12, strangely enough. Bands set to perform are Thrill Jockey mainstays Trans Am, The Sea and Cake, and Tortoise. Califone, ADULT, and The Fiery Furnaces will also round out the event. The label commissioned Girl Talk’s Greg Gillis to create a mastermix that spans the label’s entire history to be given out at the show. Additionally, to celebrate its years of indie service, plans are underway for a 7″ box set with label artists covering each other’s music.
