Avengers reunite, tour

Famous mainly for opening for the Sex Pistols at Winterland on the last date of the Pistols’ first American tour, The Avengers were as punk rock as you could get. The band never even released an album during its life span, which was only about two years (1977-1979). The band’s rudimentary punk sparked enough interest for several posthumous releases, however, the best of which was the self-titled compilation LP in 1983, also known as the “pink album” (which is way out of print, but you can download it for 10 bucks here). And since no band is exempt from the reunion disease, two original members of the Avengers (vocalist Penelope Houston and guitarist Greg Ingraham) have regrouped to put together some sort of tour. I’m not sure that this will “cash in” exactly, but one can never underestimate the power of punk rock nostalgia.

SELLOUT: Band of Horses makes deal with devil

I realize it’s some times shitty to be in a touring band. You don’t really make that much money, unless your selling out stadiums the world over. Everybody’s downloading your album, so your CD’s just collect dust at the merch table. But, artists are supposed to have it rough. My sympathy only extends so far. But it seems that lately more and more people are relenting and actually feeling sorry for bands and their income plights, even overlooking the occasional blatant sellout. I’ve never been a fan of bands shilling for corporations. It’s just tacky. And it makes you respect the ones who you know will never let you down even more. Like Tom Waits. Total class act. The man wouldn’t dream of loaning his music to Target or McDonald’s. But some of the younger bands aren’t quite so discerning.

Prince can give his records away for free, but ain’t nobody else gonna do it

Prince plans to sue YouTube for its lackluster filtering practices in an effort to “reclaim his art.” And while he’s at it, eBay and The Pirate Bay are on his list, too. If he’s on a quest to become the new Metallica, then this might be a good idea. But Metallica’s notorious bitching and complaining about piracy has only made them look like spoiled pansies over the years. And spoiled pansies do not make good records. What up, St. Anger? Granted, Prince isn’t one to give much of an ass about his public perception, especially when it comes to money. But his ‘n’ hers sets of gold-flecked purple underwear are not cheap.
