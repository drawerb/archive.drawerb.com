A Camp “Stronger Than Jesus” live at Swedish Grammys

A Camp is Nina Persson of The Cardigans as well as Atomic Swing’s Niclas Frisk and her husband Nathan Larson of Shudder to Think. Colonia is the band’s second record (out April, 28th 2009 on Nettwerk), slowly following 2001’s self-titled debut. To a casual observer, Persson’s home away from The Cardigans would seem like drastic change of direction, unless you’ve followed The Cardigans into adulthood, in which case mid-tempo balladeering will sound just about right. No matter what group she’s fronting, Persson’s charming and girlish voice is the centerpiece, and she handles the less than chirpy moods with casual aplomb.

R.I.P. The Cramps’ Lux Interior October 21, 1946 – February 4, 2009

A founder and leader of The Cramps for decades, Lux Interior has died at age 62 of an existing heart condition. My introduction to The Cramps came by way of my roommate throughout my college years. He had an entire room in his house dedicated to the band, as well as an extensive collection of records and videos. “The Cramps Room” was covered in vintage posters of some of the band’s most famous shows. I had an opportunity to see The Cramps when I was a junior in high school, but my parents checked out who I was going to see and immediately refused to let me go. Ha. I can’t imagine why. The Guardian’s obituary.

Enter the lotusflow3r

No stranger to cryptic, inscrutable websites, the reigning king of halftime shows, Prince, has launched yet another web venture at lotusflow3r.com. After waiting about ten minutes for nothing to happen, I signed up, but have no idea what that means because I was only told “Ur In The System.” Super. So, now what do I do? Well, there are three new tunes represented by cartoon cassettes that, if clicked, will load into an old school boombox and play. There’s also some weird video thing that is a major system hog. More to come, I guess. But based on Prince’s launch party last night, you’d think it were streaming golden balloons right out of the monitor. 50 lucky guests were invited to Prince’s basement in Beverly Hills to bear witness to an intimate show. Covers were on Prince’s mind, including The Cars’ “Let’s Go”, The Rolling Stones’ “Miss You”, The Doobie Brothers’ “Long Train Running”, Mary J. Blige’s “Be Happy” and a Sly & the Family Stone medley. I’d love to see footage of that Cars cover.
