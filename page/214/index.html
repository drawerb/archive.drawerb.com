Sub Pop opens virtual mom & pop

Seattle’s Sub Pop Records is taking digital distribution into its own hands with a new mp3 shop. Its current online catalog is running 200 albums deep. No individual track purchases are available yet, and the encoding is only 192kbps. But, Sub Pop is open to criticism and welcomes user feedback to help improve the store. Albums are priced at $9.90 each with a collection spanning Nirvana, Mudhoney, St. Etienne, and Six Finger Satellite through current releases from Band of Horses and Iron and Wine. Bitrates are scheduled to improve and PDF artwork packages will soon be available. It’s much easier to wade through albums from a trusted label than feeling overwhelmed at a search bar by a clusterfucky behemoth like iTunes.

Elvis Costello reunites with Clover for charity

Clover, the little-known backing band that played on Elvis Costello’s debut, My Aim Is True, some thirty years ago is reuniting with Costello for the first time since they all left the studio when the album wrapped. The cause is for Costello’s long-time friend and fellow musician, Austin De Lone, whose son suffers from “Prader-Willi syndrome, a chromosome disorder that affects 1 in 15,000 births and leaves its victims perpetually starving.” The event spans two sold out night at San Francisco’s Great American Music Hall, where the reunited musicians will play My Aim Is True in its entirety. Even if he won’t play his home country ever again, Costello hasn’t lost touch with his past.

New My Bloody Valentine music by year’s end?

More less-than-reliable news has surfaced claiming that things might be brewing in the My Bloody Valentine camp. Billboard is sourcing the Daily Swarm with the scoop that Kevin Shields has indirectly confirmed that My Bloody Valentine will release new music by the end of this year. He appeared in an interview segment with The Make-Up’s Ian Svenonious for vbs.tv. This smacks of Chinese Democracy-level bullshit, but I must admit I have way more faith in Shields’ word than that of Axl Rose’s. According to the Daily Swarm a new MBV record is 3/4 of the way done, which dates back to 1996. As for the Coachella rumors, they remain only rumors, though Billboard claims they have word that the negotiations have recently soured.

Pete Doherty upset heroin video leaked

News of Pete Doherty’s recent “recovery” from drug addiction was taken with exaggerated rolls of the eyes the world over. It was more than a little disingenuous to expect people to buy news that Britain’s most willfully notorious junkie was suddenly all cleaned up. Doherty has forced cynicism upon his press with blatant and comical setbacks in his illustrious road to recovery, schlepping in and out of rehab, failing court-ordered drug tests, forcing cats to smoke crack. You know, the usual. So, now that we know he’s still riding the horse full steam ahead, the balance of the world seems steady again.
