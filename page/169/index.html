Staying “indie”…

Gawker’s Hamilton Nolan correctly calls for a moratorium on the term “indie”, especially when applied to musicians who willfully seek out any possible cross-market corporate tie-in in order to sustain so-called “independence.” Or as Nolan succinctly states:

…to sell a single freaking song in today’s environment, musicians must rush around bootlicking every monster corporation of any type willing to give away some airplay and free promotion.

The Wall Street Journal’s sample “indie” artist is Greg Laswell, whose label, Vanguard, plans to have Laswell’s music piped into movie chains and hotel lobbies all in the name of getting the word out by circumventing the normal channels. Or, to simplify, because no one actually would care otherwise.

His list of embarrassing buzz word nonsense promotional gimmicks includes posters in Whole Foods’ grocery checkout lines where customers see him “compared to EMI Group’s Coldplay.” Sold!

Shudder to Think reunion continues into mini fall tour

Shudder to Think vocalist Craig Wedren recently told Billboard that he and guitarist Nathan Larson have put aside all of the “venom and puss” that would preclude a reunion so as to move forward with playing together again. I’m not sure what sort of concoction venom and puss is exactly, but it sounds unpleasant. Nevertheless, after some prodding last fall by Rain Phoenix of all people, Shudder to Think played an impromptu show at the Mercury Lounge in NYC. Now the band has upped the ante for a small-scale tour of both coasts this fall. Things kick off at the Virgin Mobile Fesival in Baltimore mid-August. Depending on how you look at it, the band’s last album was either 50,000 B.C. or the soundtrack for the film First Love, Last Rights back in 1998.

Lily Allen might quit music if you don’t like her new record

Lily Allen is one party with Pete Doherty away from Amy Winehouse-ing her career. Since the success of her debut, Alright, Sill, Allen has made herself more than available for tabloid fodder. Mosty drunken shenanigans. Nothing too crazy. Luckily for her, Amy Winehouse’s spiral stairs are waaaay steeper, so Allen’s hijinks have mostly been forgettable. Her current spat with fat talentless fucktard Perez Hilton aside, Allen has made some curious choices post-fame.  She’s a perpetual over-sharer on her MySpace blog, lamenting cliched body-type issues and generic insecurities, the sappiness of which are usually more than made up for in caustic wit. But her latest self-esteem crisis is her morbid fear of the public’s reception to her forthcoming album, tentatively titled Stuck on the Naughty Step, as she recently told Rolling Stone:

I’m really scared about what people are going to say. Some people will like it and some won’t. If people hate it, then I’ll just try something else.

Bah.

Chromeo “Fancy Footwork” on Jimmy Kimmel Live 6-17-08

This is completely awesome. Taking cues from Robert Palmer’s notoriously expressionless mannequins (and, perhaps, the “prize-winning” lamp from A Christmas Story), Chromeo does the late Palmer one up by foregoing expressions altogether, as a dozen pair of illuminated legs dance subtly in the background. While Chromeo unleashes its nerdy white boy synth funk, these torso-less women kick up some beats on the snare. The song ain’t half bad either. Now I want to see Chromeo live.

Abba turns collective nose up at reunion frenzy

Who knew Abba had such strict principles when it came to cashing in on the reunion craze? Lead songwriter Bjorn Ulvaeus told the Sunday Telegraph:

We will never appear on stage again. There is simply no motivation to re-group. Money is not a factor and we would like people to remember us as we were – young, exuberant, full of energy and ambition.

I remember Robert Plant saying Led Zeppelin were a cover band now because they cover all their own stuff. I think that hit the nail on the head.
