RETRO: Assfactor 4 “Sometimes I Suck” mp3

Assfactor 4 finally has a Myspace page with four songs posted, spanning early 7″ work through its final, posthumous LP, Sports. Sadly, there’s not much available on the web about these guys. AF4 was one of the best hardcore bands of the early to mid-90’s, or maybe, ever. Spastic, screaming vocals, dueling, buzzsaw guitars, and frenetic drumming all haphazardly thrown together in short, melodic bursts. If the aural cacaphony is off-putting at first, keep listening. The hooks are undeniable and infectious. This band put out a handful of 7″s and two full-lengths on various hardcore labels in its brief career, playing its final show in July of 1997.

In it for the money, Dinosaur Jr. and Sebadoh plan new tours

With the imminent release of Beyond on Fat Possum records on May 1st, the first Dinosaur Jr. album featuring the original trio of J. Mascis, Lou Barlow, and Murph since 1989, the band is hitting the road again on May 11th. Annoyingly, there are only ten cities on the itinerary, thus far. The usual New York, Chicago, and Hollywood, blah, blah, blah. No love for the south, of course.

I’m astounded Lou and J. have gotten along well enough to complete a record, much less more than one tour. Barlow is so agreeable these days he’s even patched things up with Eric Gaffney and Jason Lowenstein, reuniting his post-Dinosaur Jr. band Sebadoh for a spring tour. No recording plans yet, though, says Gaffney: “We’re just going to get through this (tour) and see where we’re at.”

Duran Duran scrapped entire album

When Andy Taylor quit the band last October (for the second time), Duran Duran scrapped an entire album’s worth of material.  I’m not sure why they couldn’t have released it anyway, but the band decided to start from scratch.

Says keyboardist Nick Rhodes: “The album had to be scrapped when five became four. We started again and now we’re nearly there.” He also said the band hopes to collaborate with Nelly Furtado.  Great. First, it was Justin Timberlake (which is certainly why Andy Taylor quit and props to him for having a spine). Then it was Timbaland. And now Nelly Furtado? Jesus. How much can they whore it up with the whole “current” guest star bit? It never works, and it’s an embarrassing grasp for late-career relevance. 

 No one wants to hear Duran Duran with hip hop beats.  Its Public Enemy cover of “911 Is  A Joke” off 1995’s ill-advised Thank You album already has the esteemed position of number 1 worst cover song of all time.
